One Hour Reading-Day 2

  • English
  • OHR

罗琳用词简单但不失丰富性，在阅读哈利波特的过程中可以学到很多小词，但是英美人士十分常用的词。

经常看见一个现象：地点状语后面加be动词的倒装形式。

Perched on the top of the rock was the most miserable little shack you could imagine.

上面的句子使用倒装方式，状语提前。
be perched = be situated = be positioned = be sited 坐落于

Obviously he thought nobody stood a chance of reaching them here in a storm to deliver mail.

stand a chance : 有希望，有机会

Aunt Petunia found a few mouldy blankets in the second room and made up a bed for Dudley on the moth-eaten sofa.

moth-eaten : old and in bad condition
mouldy: 发霉的

Harry lay and watched his birthday tick nearer, wondering if the Dursleys would remember at all, wondering where the letter writer was now.

分词做状语的用法。

Harry heard something creak outside.

if something such as a door, wooden floor, old bed, or stairs creaks, it makes a long high noise when someone opens it.

The door creaked open.

What was the funny crunching noise?

The whole shack shivered and Harry sat bolt upright, staring at the door.

sit /stand bolt upright: to sit or stand with your back very straight.

Dudley jerked awake.

猛地惊醒

The door was hit with such force that it swung clean off its hinges and with a deafening crash landed flat on the floor.

land flat on the floor
land这里指落下。

deafening: very loud. 震耳欲聋的

非常寂静：deafening silence, 很奇怪，deafening代表很响，这两个连用很矛盾。

People living near airports suffer the deafening sound of aircraft taking off and landing.

The gaint squeezed his way into the hut(shack), stooping so that his head just brushed the ceiling. He bent down, picked up the door, and fitted it easily back into frame.

stoop: bend one’s head or body forwards and downwards 俯下身子
He stooped down and reached towards the coin.
